# Example Decision Quality Checklist

## Decision under review

Launch a fixed-fee AI automation readiness audit as the first public offer.

## Goal

Secure one paid pilot and create reusable proof for future engagements.

## Mission and stewardship alignment

The pilot should create practical value for the customer by reducing confusion, waste, and unsafe automation choices. The protected boundary is to avoid overpromising, avoid sensitive-data exposure, and keep implementation claims limited to what is actually delivered.

## Evidence

Potential buyers express confusion about AI automation and concern about safe implementation. The offer can be delivered through reusable templates.

## Scorecard

| Area | Score | Evidence | Risk if wrong | Mitigation |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Customer value | 4 | Clear pain around AI confusion and workflow waste. | Buyer may not prioritize now. | Start with warm contacts. |
| Cash impact | 4 | Fixed fee and limited scope. | Time may exceed estimate. | Cap deliverables and collect upfront. |
| Margin impact | 3 | Good if delivered within 12 to 25 hours. | Custom requests may erode margin. | Define exclusions. |
| Operational readiness | 3 | Templates exist but sample report needed. | Delivery may feel improvised. | Build report outline before outreach. |
| Timing | 4 | AI adoption pressure is current. | Buyer fatigue from AI hype. | Emphasize practical risk-controlled value. |
| Trust / governance | 3 | Public-safe language and disclaimers exist. | Security claims could be overread. | Avoid guarantees and regulated advice. |
| Learning / review plan | 5 | Pilot will end with AAR and template updates. | Lessons may not be captured. | Schedule review date now. |

## Assumptions

The buyer wants a readiness audit before implementation. The offer is understandable in one page. The pilot can be delivered without custom software development.

## Risks

Scope creep, unclear buyer urgency, and need for stronger proof.

## Alternatives

Publish a free checklist first, run a free discovery workshop, or build a larger implementation package.

## Kill criteria

If three qualified conversations produce no interest in a paid audit, revise the offer or segment.

## Final decision

Go, with scope control.

## Review date

Review after three conversations or the first paid pilot.
