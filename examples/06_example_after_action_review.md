# Example After-Action Review

## Context

- Date: 2026-06-25
- Decision / event / launch: Product production forecast review
- Owner: Operations lead
- Team: Strategy, finance, production, sales

## Objective

Match output to real demand while protecting cash and margin.

## Mission and stewardship alignment

The decision was intended to serve customers reliably while stewarding production capacity and cash. The drift was overconfidence in demand, which trapped resources that could have been protected through stronger forecasting discipline.

## Expected result

The team expected demand to grow and planned production accordingly.

## Actual result

Demand was lower than expected, creating excess inventory and carrying cost.

## Evidence

Sales volume was lower than forecast. Inventory remained after the period ended. Carrying cost reduced margin.

## Cause analysis

The team relied on a general growth expectation instead of the most current demand signal. The team also failed to test a downside case before setting production.

## What worked

The team reviewed the result, identified the forecasting error, and adjusted future production.

## What failed or underperformed

Forecasting discipline was weak. The team did not test downside demand and did not fully connect production volume to inventory carrying cost.

## Assumptions tested

- Confirmed: Product quality alone does not guarantee sell-through.
- Weakened: Normal growth rate was a reliable planning assumption.
- Disproven: Producing more is safer when the product is strong.

## Business-system impact

- Service / Customer: Product quality improved, but market demand was lower than assumed.
- Stewardship / Resources: Cash was trapped in inventory.
- Cash: Carrying cost reduced flexibility.
- Margin / Economics: Excess inventory weakened margins.
- Timing: Production decision was made before validating current demand.
- Forecasting: Forecast source was insufficient.
- Inventory / Capacity: Production exceeded credible demand.
- Operations / Quality: Production execution worked, but planning quality failed.
- Trust / Governance: Decision process needs stronger evidence review.
- Learning Loop: Lesson should become a forecast checklist rule.

## Lessons learned

Forecast from evidence, not hope. Production must match real demand, not ambition. A strong product can still create weak results if timing, demand, and inventory are wrong.

## System update

Before production decisions, complete the [Forecast Assumptions Register Template](../templates/03_forecast_assumptions_register_template.md) and review base, downside, and upside cases.

## Owner and due date

- Owner: Operations lead
- Due date: Before next production decision
- Follow-up review date: Next operating review
