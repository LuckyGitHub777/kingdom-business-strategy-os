# Example Business Model Review

## Offer

AI automation readiness audit for a small professional-services firm.

## Customer

Owner-led firms with 10 to 50 employees that use repeated manual admin workflows and want practical AI help without losing control of data or quality.

## Problem

The team wastes time on repetitive document handling, intake, follow-ups, and status reporting. Leadership knows AI matters but does not know which workflows are safe to automate first.

## Value

The customer receives a ranked automation map, risk flags, recommended tools, and a 30-day implementation plan.

## Evidence of demand

The customer already spends staff hours on repeated intake and reporting. The owner has asked for AI options but is worried about sensitive data and tool selection.

## Price

Fixed-fee pilot between $2,500 and $7,500 depending on scope.

## Full cost to deliver

Discovery calls, workflow mapping, tool review, risk review, report writing, and implementation planning. Delivery may require 12 to 25 hours.

## Margin

Gross margin appears healthy if scope is fixed and custom build work is excluded from the audit.

## Delivery model

Remote interviews, workflow review, template-based analysis, final executive brief, and optional implementation phase.

## Risk

Scope creep, unclear data access, client expecting full automation build instead of readiness audit, and unsupported security claims.

## Repeatability

High if the intake form, workflow map, risk checklist, and final report template are reused.

## Scale conditions

Need standardized intake, delivery checklist, sample report, and clear public-use boundary.

## Mission and stewardship alignment

The offer should help the customer reduce waste, make safer AI decisions, and protect sensitive information. The boundary is clear: the audit identifies readiness and risks; it does not promise regulated security assurance, legal advice, or custom automation delivery unless separately scoped.

## Decision

Continue.
