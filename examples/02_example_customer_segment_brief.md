# Example Customer Segment Brief

## Segment name

Owner-led professional-services firms adopting AI.

## Buyer description

Founder, managing partner, operations leader, or general manager at a 10 to 50 person service business.

## Core problem

Manual workflows consume time, but the buyer does not know which AI automations are useful, safe, or worth implementing.

## Buying criteria

Most important criteria:

- Practicality
- Trust
- Security
- Speed
- Evidence
- Clear implementation path

## Budget and willingness to pay

Likely willing to pay for a focused audit if it prevents wasted software spend or saves staff time.

## Proof required

Sample workflow map, sample risk flags, practical examples, and clear explanation of what is included and excluded.

## Decision process

Owner or managing partner decides. Operations manager influences. IT/security advisor may block risky tool choices.

## Timing window

Usually after the buyer has experimented with AI tools, felt confusion, or seen competitors adopt AI.

## Alternatives

Do nothing, ask staff to experiment, buy generic software, hire a consultant, or follow vendor recommendations.

## Message

We identify the safest and highest-value workflows to automate first, then give leadership a practical plan they can control.

## Risks

The segment may expect implementation, underbudget the work, or lack clean internal process documentation.
